01Three moments across every project

Contract signing · Day one
Set the expectation.
"One more thing before we get rolling. When we wrap and you're happy with the work, I'm going to ask you for a Google review. It's how most of our new clients find us. Just want you to know that's part of how we close out every project."
Mid-project · Milestone day
The check-in.
"Almost there. Once we wrap the final details you're going to get a text from me with that Google review link we talked about. Nothing heavy, just one tap."
Final walkthrough · Job close-out
The ask + same-day text.
"Last thing. Like I mentioned on day one, I'm going to text you a Google review link tonight. It's the single thing that helps me the most. Totally fine if something comes up and you can't get to it."
SMS (evening of): "Hey [name] this is [your name] with [company]. Thanks again for having us out. Here's the Google review link I mentioned: [short URL]. Appreciate you."

Crew bonus + the 2026 rules.

Pay the crew for effort, not the customer for reviews. That keeps you clean with Google and it actually works.

03Plug-and-play crew bonus

Baseline structure

  • $20-$40 per landed review (paid to the crew member tied to the project)
  • $100 quarterly bonus to the crew member with the most reviews (or split if the team is small)
  • Public leaderboard in the shop — reviews landed by crew, month over month
  • Paid monthly, on a regular payroll cycle (not cash, not a gift card)

The rules that keep it legal under Google's March 2026 update

  • OK  Paying crew for doing the ask correctly and the customer freely leaving a review
  • OK  Bonuses tied to review count, not specific star ratings
  • OK  Internal leaderboards, recognition, bonuses for crew effort
  • NO  Offering the customer anything in exchange (discounts, tips back, perks)
  • NO  Paying bonuses only on 5-star reviews (that's review gating)
  • NO  Having the crew leave reviews themselves or ask family to leave reviews

04Don't do this (2026 update)

  • No incentives to the customer. No discounts, gift cards, raffle entries, free add-ons in exchange for reviews. Retroactive enforcement — old ones can get pulled.
  • No employee or subcontractor reviews. Not your crew, not the guys you sub to regularly, not office staff. Google cross-references accounts.
  • No family reviews. Your cousin, brother-in-law, kids. The profile gets flagged.
  • No AI-generated review text for the customer to paste. Google's detector catches the content itself.
  • No review gating. Asking "would this be 5 stars?" and only sending the yes group to Google is explicitly banned.
  • No bulk third-party services. "Get 20 reviews in a week" operations got wiped in the update.
