Review request scripts that don't sound desperate.

Five copy-paste scripts for the three project moments where real contractor reviews actually come from. No incentives. No gating. No AI-sounding filler. Built on the playbook from the Google reviews post.

How to use this pack.

Tap the COPY button on any template. Paste it into your CRM, your notes app, or a text to the customer. Swap the placeholders in brackets ([name], [your name], [company], [short URL]) for your real info.

These go with the three-moment framework from the how-to-get-more-google-reviews post. Contract signing. Mid-project milestone. Final walkthrough. Same-day text. Day-3 fallback.

01 · Contract signing — set the expectation.

Plant the review expectation on day one, during the paperwork walkthrough. Not a pitch, not an ask. Just a heads-up that closing a project includes a review.

In person · At contract signing Spoken
One more thing before we get rolling. When we wrap up and you're happy with the work, I'm going to ask you for a Google review. It's how most of our new clients find us. Just want you to know that's part of how we close out every project.

02 · Mid-project milestone — the check-in.

Every trade has a visual-payoff moment before the job is done. The day the pavers go down, cabinets get hung, pool gets filled, tile gets set. That's your priming touch. You're keeping the commitment warm, not closing it.

In person · On site, milestone day Spoken
Almost there. Once we wrap the final details you're going to get a text from me with that Google review link we talked about. Nothing heavy, just one tap.

03 · Final walkthrough — the ask.

The formal ask lives inside the walkthrough close-out, same breath as warranty info and care instructions. Giving the customer an out (last sentence) lifts follow-through because nobody likes pressure.

In person · Walkthrough Spoken
Last thing. Like I mentioned on day one, I'm going to text you a Google review link tonight. It's the single thing that helps me the most. Totally fine if something comes up and you can't get to it.

04 · Same-day text — the follow-through.

Send this the evening of the walkthrough. From your actual number, not a marketing blast. Short link — nobody taps a 60-character Google URL.

SMS · Evening of job completion Text
Hey [name] this is [your name] with [company]. Thanks again for having us out. Here's the Google review link I mentioned: [short URL]. Appreciate you.

05 · Day-3 email — the safety net.

If the review hasn't landed three days after job close-out, send this one short email. Subject line is the hook. Plain text, your real signature.

Email · 3 days after job complete Written
Subject: One quick favor Hey [name], Hope the [patio / system / roof / project] is working well for you. No pressure, but if you have 60 seconds, a Google review would mean a lot: [short URL] Thanks, [your name]

What not to do in 2026.

Google's March 2026 policy update pulled a bunch of old tactics off the board. None of these belong in any of the templates above.

  • No incentives. No discounts, gift cards, tips, raffle entries, anything of value in exchange for a review.
  • No employee, subcontractor, or family reviews. Google cross-references reviewer accounts.
  • No bulk third-party services. The "get 20 reviews in a week" operations got wiped in the update.
  • No AI-generated review text for the customer to paste. The content itself is detected.
  • No review gating. Don't pre-screen happy vs unhappy and only send the happy ones to Google.
