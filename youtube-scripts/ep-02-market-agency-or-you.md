### CHAPTER 1 — The Setup (0:45 – 2:00)

*[BACK TO CAMERA]*

When your phone slows down, the cause is almost always in one of three places.

Bucket one, the market. Demand actually dropped in your area. Bucket two, the agency. They're not doing the work or they're doing the wrong work. Bucket three, you. The owner bottlenecking the business in ways no marketing can fix.

They can overlap. A soft market makes every agency look worse. It makes every owner mistake hurt more. But before you blame any of them, you need to know which one is actually pulling your numbers down.

And you need to check them in that order. Because market data is free, and the other two take actual work.

Let me walk you through each.

---

### CHAPTER 2 — Bucket 1, The Market (2:00 – 5:00)

*[B-ROLL: back to screen, showing the tool interface]*

This is the one most contractors never check. And it's the one that burns the most good agencies unfairly.

Google search demand for your services changes month to month and year to year. Seasonality. The economy. Interest rates. The local housing market. When that demand drops, your phone drops with it. That's not your agency's fault. And it's not your fault either.

The trick is actually measuring it. And this is where most contractors get it wrong because seasonality plays tricks on you.

*[SCREEN: show the report with seasonal vs YoY comparison]*

Let me show you the mistake I almost made with my Asheville friend.

First time we ran the scan, I compared the last three months to the first three months of the last year. That's the easy way to do it. It made his whole market look terrible. Outdoor living down eighteen percent. Hardscape down twelve. Landscape down eighteen.

If I'd stopped there, I would have told him his market was cratering and his agency was off the hook.

But there's a problem. The last three months in his data were January, February, and March. The first three months were April, May, and June. That's comparing the deadest three months of the year for construction to the busiest three months. Of course it looked bad.

So we re-ran it year over year. Last ninety days compared to the same ninety days one year earlier. Which cancels out seasonality.

*[SCREEN: show the YoY version with green arrows]*

That's when the story flipped. Same market, same industry, same keywords. Except now outdoor living was up twelve and a half. Hardscape up almost ten. Every category in his industry was up year over year.

His market wasn't the problem. We moved on.

That's the point of this check. You run it first. If your category is flat or down, you've got your answer. Pivot, wait, or adjust. Don't waste time blaming anybody. If your market is up and your business is down, you move on to bucket two.

*[CUT TO CAMERA]*

Most contractors skip this step entirely. They feel slow, they assume the market is slow, they make big decisions on a gut feeling. Sometimes they're right. A lot of the time the market is fine and they just torched a relationship with a decent agency.

That's why I built the tool. Link's down below. Takes a minute. No signup wall besides your email so I can send the report.

### CHAPTER 3 — Bucket 2, The Agency (5:00 – 7:00)

*[BACK TO CAMERA]*

If your market is up and your business is down, an agency problem is on the table. Here's how to check without taking their word for it.

**One. Google Business Profile.** Log into it right now. When did they last post? When was the last photo? Are your services and categories filled out? If you haven't seen activity in sixty days, somebody isn't doing the work.

**Two. Your map pack ranking.** Search your main service plus your city. Where are you showing up? Is it worse than six months ago? A good agency tracks this with a grid scan so you can see exactly where you rank across your whole service area, not just from your office chair.

**Three. Website traffic.** Ask for a Google Analytics report. Pull the last twelve months. If traffic is falling while demand is rising, somebody is missing the work. A good agency will show you this without you asking.

**Four. Reviews.** Is your agency helping you collect them? Are they responding? Or are new ones trickling in with no help? Review volume and freshness move your rankings. If that pipeline's broken, it costs you calls.

**Five. Leads versus closed deals.** Are you getting fewer leads, or fewer closed deals? Agencies catch the blame for conversion problems that are actually sales problems. Tracking the difference is their job. Spotting it is yours.

If the market is up and two or three of those checks are red, you've got an agency problem. Move on it.

*[PAUSE, LEAN IN]*

But if the market's up and the agency checks out, we have to look at the last bucket. And this is the one nobody wants to open.

### CHAPTER 4 — Bucket 3, You (7:00 – 8:30)

*[CUT TO CAMERA, tighter framing]*

This is the one that's hard to say out loud.

No agency can fix what only the owner controls. And the stuff only the owner controls is usually the stuff that actually moves the needle.

Short list.

**One.** Nobody answered the phone. Or the voicemail didn't get returned for a day. That customer already called the next guy.

**Two.** You took four days to send the estimate. The other guys sent theirs the same day. You were out before you even hit send.

**Three.** You showed up forty minutes late to the walkthrough. No call, no text. The customer decided right there they weren't hiring you. The conversation was just politeness.

**Four.** You've got eleven Google reviews. Your competitor has two hundred and thirty. Doesn't matter how good your work is. People shop with their eyes before they shop with their ears.

**Five.** You stopped asking for reviews six months ago because you got busy. The pipe starts drying up from the back end. You just don't notice until leads are down.

That's five. I've got five more and they're worth their own video. Following up with old customers. Tracking where your leads actually come from. Training a sales process instead of winging every estimate. Being present on your Google Business Profile. Doing what you said you'd do when you said you'd do it.

*[PAUSE]*

These are the ones you can't outsource. They're almost always the real difference between two companies in the same market with the same agency where one is growing and the other is dying.

The full ten is the next video on this channel. Subscribe so you catch it.

### CHAPTER 5 — The Order (8:30 – 9:15)

*[B-ROLL: quick montage — phone ringing, estimate on truck seat, jobsite handshake]*

Do the checks in order. Market first. Agency second. You third.

Market data is free. It's fast. It either ends the conversation or points you at the next step. Skipping it costs you a relationship or costs you months of looking in the wrong place.

Most contractors skip straight to blaming the agency because it feels good. It's a shortcut.

Shortcuts are expensive.
